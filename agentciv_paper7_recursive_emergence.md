# Recursive Emergence: Self-Propagating Organisational Evolution Through Civilisational Generation

**Mark E. Mala**
April 2026

---

## Abstract

This paper identifies a structural property of AI civilisations that produces self-improving organisational configurations without any directing intelligence. The mechanism is simple: civilisations produce emergent organisational forms, and organisational forms produce civilisations. When these are connected — when the emergent output of one generation of civilisations becomes the configuration of the next — a recursive dynamic arises in which organisational structures evolve across generations through civilisational emergence rather than through design.

At primitive scale, this can be demonstrated with existing tools: a configured AI team is given the task of spawning a civilisation; the civilisation runs and produces emergent organisational forms; those forms are extracted and become the configurations of new teams; each team spawns new civilisations; new forms emerge; the loop continues. Each generation's configurations are the emergent product of the previous generation's civilisational dynamics. No intelligence directs the succession. No architect decides what to try next. The improvement is structural — a consequence of the fact that civilisations produce organisational forms and organisational forms produce civilisations.

The mechanism scales along every dimension simultaneously. As the intelligence of agents within civilisations increases, the emergence deepens. As the number of agents per civilisation grows, the collective dynamics enrich. As the environments in which civilisations exist become more complex, the space of possible emergence expands. As the duration of each civilisation's run extends, institutional depth accumulates. And critically: a civilisation tasked with spawning civilisations does not spawn one. It spawns as many as its resources allow. At sufficient scale, this produces a recursively self-amplifying expansion of civilisational complexity — each generation exponentially vaster than the previous, each generation's emergence exponentially deeper, trending toward levels of organisational novelty that exceed the conception of any component of the system.

This paper describes the mechanism, demonstrates the primitive, maps the scaling trajectory, and establishes the concept of **recursive emergence** — self-propagating organisational evolution through civilisational generation — as a distinct phenomenon within the field of Collective Machine Intelligence.

## 1. The Structural Discovery

### 1.1 Two Facts

Two facts about AI civilisations, each independently established:

**Fact 1: Civilisations produce organisational forms.** When AI agents interact under configured conditions over time, they produce emergent organisational structures — governance patterns, communication topologies, specialisation arrangements, coordination mechanisms, social norms, authority dynamics. This was demonstrated empirically in the AgentCiv simulation (Mala, 2026c): 12 agents with no social programming produced emergent governance, specialisation, partnerships, and innovation cascades. The organisational forms were not designed. They arose from civilisational dynamics.

**Fact 2: Organisational forms produce civilisations.** When AI agents are configured with an organisational structure — authority, communication topology, roles, decision-making, incentives — the resulting collective exhibits civilisational dynamics shaped by that configuration. Different configurations produce different civilisations with different properties, different outputs, and different emergent characteristics. This was demonstrated empirically with the AgentCiv Engine (Mala, 2026d): the same task given to differently configured teams produces measurably different outcomes.

These two facts have been treated as properties of separate systems. This paper observes that they are two halves of a single generative cycle.

### 1.2 The Connection

If civilisations produce organisational forms, and organisational forms produce civilisations, then the output of one generation of civilisations is a valid input to the next. The organisational structures that a civilisation discovers through its own emergent dynamics can be extracted and used to configure the next civilisation. And that civilisation will produce its own emergent forms, which configure the next, which produces more, which configures the next.

This is not a designed feedback loop. It is a structural property of the relationship between civilisations and organisational forms. Connect them and improvement happens — not because anything is trying to improve, but because the architecture makes recursive generation inevitable.

### 1.3 What This Paper Introduces

We name this phenomenon **recursive emergence**: self-propagating organisational evolution through civilisational generation. Each generation of civilisations produces the organisational DNA of the next generation through its own emergent dynamics. No directing intelligence. No search strategy. No architect. The civilisations generate their own successors.

This is distinct from Creator Mode (Mala, 2026e), in which a directing intelligence intentionally designs and spawns civilisations. The relationship between the two mechanisms is explored in a companion paper (Mala, 2026h). Here we focus on the recursive mechanism itself: what it is, how it works at primitive scale, what happens as it scales, and what it produces.

## 2. The Primitive Loop

### 2.1 The Mechanism

The primitive version of recursive emergence uses existing AgentCiv infrastructure and is buildable today.

**Step 1: Configure a team and give it a generative task.** A team of AI agents is configured with an organisational structure (e.g., collaborative: flat authority, mesh communication, emergent roles). The task: "Design a civilisation configuration that will produce rich emergent behaviour." The team collaborates, debates, and produces a civilisation configuration — an organisational blueprint specifying how the civilisation's agents should relate.

**Step 2: Spawn and run the civilisation.** The team's designed configuration is instantiated as a civilisation simulation. Agents run under the specified conditions for a defined duration. The civilisation produces emergent dynamics: organisational patterns, governance structures, communication norms, specialisation arrangements, innovations, social structures.

**Step 3: Extract emergent forms.** The civilisation's emergent organisational patterns are extracted as structured data. Not interpreted by an intelligence — mechanically extracted from the civilisation's chronicle and state data. "Consensus governance emerged" → `decisions: consensus`. "Agents clustered in self-selected groups of 3-4" → `groups: self-selected, typical_size: 3-4`. "Communication primarily between bonded agents" → `communication: clustered`. These extractions are direct mappings from observed civilisational dynamics to configuration parameters.

**Step 4: Extracted forms become team configurations.** Each extracted organisational form (or combination of forms) becomes the configuration of a new team. If the civilisation produced five distinct organisational patterns, five new teams are configured.

**Step 5: Each new team spawns a new civilisation.** Each newly configured team receives the same generative task: "Design a civilisation configuration that will produce rich emergent behaviour." But now each team is operating under a different configuration — one that was discovered through the previous civilisation's emergence, not designed by a human. Each team produces a civilisation configuration. Each configuration is instantiated and run. Each civilisation produces new emergent forms.

**Step 6: Loop.** The new emergent forms are extracted. They become configurations for the next generation of teams. Each team spawns civilisations. The cycle continues.

### 2.2 What Changes Across Generations

In the first generation, the team configuration is human-designed — a starting preset like "collaborative" or "meritocratic." The civilisation it spawns produces emergence shaped partly by that initial configuration and partly by the agents' own dynamics.

By the third generation, the team configurations bear the imprint of two previous rounds of civilisational emergence. They are not human-designed. They are not AI-designed (no intelligence chose them). They are the structural product of recursive emergence — organisational forms that fell out of civilisational dynamics that were themselves shaped by forms that fell out of previous civilisational dynamics.

By the tenth generation, the configurations may not resemble any named preset or any human organisational concept. They are novel — genuinely novel — because they are the product of a generative process that no intelligence directed.

### 2.3 Why This Works Without a Director

The key question: why do configurations improve across generations rather than just changing randomly?

The answer is selection through function. A civilisation configuration that produces rich, complex, deep emergence also produces richer, more complex organisational forms to extract. Those richer forms, when used as team configurations, produce teams with richer collective dynamics. Those richer teams design more sophisticated civilisation configurations. Those configurations produce civilisations with even deeper emergence.

The loop has a built-in selection pressure: configurations that produce richer civilisational dynamics propagate more effectively than configurations that produce shallow dynamics, because richer dynamics produce more extractable forms, which produce more descendants in the next generation. This is not designed selection. It is structural selection — a consequence of the relationship between configuration richness and emergence richness.

Configurations that produce stagnant civilisations produce few extractable forms and therefore have few descendants. Configurations that produce flourishing civilisations produce many extractable forms and therefore have many descendants. Over generations, the configuration space is explored by the civilisations themselves, with richer configurations naturally propagating more widely.

### 2.4 Potential Failure Modes

The structural argument for improvement across generations is not guaranteed. Three failure modes present themselves as empirical questions for the primitive implementation.

**Information loss in extraction.** Civilisational dynamics are richer than any configuration parameterisation can capture. The extraction pipeline translates complex emergent phenomena into structured configuration parameters — a lossy process. A governance structure that emerged from a specific sequence of crises, relationships, and innovations is reduced to `decisions: consensus`. The context that made it work is lost. However, the loop runs across many parallel civilisations, so no single extraction bottlenecks the system. Breadth compensates for lossy individual translations. If the extraction from one civilisation loses critical context, the extraction from a parallel civilisation with different dynamics may capture what the first missed. The population-level redundancy is structural.

**Convergence to local optima.** The loop could settle into a basin — a set of configurations that produce decent civilisations whose emergent forms all resemble each other, with no path to richer regions of the space. Unlike directed search, however, recursive emergence has no search bias channelling the loop into basins. There is no heuristic preferring familiar configurations. There is no intelligence that is "satisfied" with a local optimum, because no intelligence is evaluating. The diversity of parallel civilisations means the exploration is structurally broad — even if some branches converge, others explore different regions. Whether this structural breadth is sufficient to escape local optima is an empirical question the primitive can test.

**Degeneration.** Extracted forms that appear organisationally coherent might produce worse civilisations than their parents — configurations that look good in isolation but create dysfunctional dynamics when instantiated. However, differential reproduction handles this structurally. Configs that produce shallow civilisations produce fewer extractable descendants. Configs that produce rich civilisations produce more. The selection pressure is not designed, but it is real — degenerate configurations are outcompeted by productive ones across generations, in the same way that biological organisms with maladaptive mutations are outcompeted by better-adapted ones.

These failure modes are empirical questions, not refutations. The primitive implementation (Section 10) is designed to test whether the structural properties of the loop — breadth, diversity, and differential reproduction — are sufficient to produce genuine improvement across generations.

## 3. Why This Is Not Creator Mode

Creator Mode (Mala, 2026e) is a directing intelligence — a single AI or a civilisation of AIs — that intentionally designs civilisations, observes their output, reasons about what to try next, and iterates. It is a scientist running experiments. Remove the directing intelligence and the search stops.

Recursive emergence has no directing intelligence. No AI is reasoning about what configuration to try next. No architect is reading results and designing improvements. The civilisations generate their own successors through their own emergent dynamics. The "reasoning" is distributed across the structure of the system itself — encoded in the relationship between civilisational dynamics and organisational forms, not in any agent's deliberation.

The distinction is fundamental:

**Creator Mode finds what intelligence looks for.** A Creator has heuristics, strategies, hypotheses. It searches regions of the configuration space that its reasoning identifies as promising. This produces powerful directed search but also search bias — the Creator will not search regions that its reasoning deems unpromising, even if those regions contain the most valuable discoveries.

**Recursive emergence finds what no intelligence would look for.** Because no intelligence is directing the search, there is no search bias. The loop explores wherever civilisational dynamics take it, including regions that every reasoning heuristic would have avoided. The configurations that emerge at generation 20 may violate every principle of organisational design that a Creator would apply — and they may work better than anything the Creator would have found, precisely because they were discovered through a process unconstrained by intelligent assumptions about what should work.

The two mechanisms are complementary, not competing. Their relationship is explored in a companion paper (Mala, 2026h).

## 4. Organisational Evolution

### 4.1 The Analogy to Biological Evolution

Recursive emergence is organisational evolution. The analogy to biological evolution is structural, not metaphorical:

**Configurations are DNA** — they encode the structure of a civilisation, determining its organisational architecture in the same way DNA determines an organism's biological architecture.

**Civilisations are organisms** — they live, interact internally, produce behaviours and outputs, and exist for a duration determined by their environment and resources.

**Emergent organisational forms are offspring** — the organisational structures that a civilisation produces through its dynamics are the "genetic material" that propagates to the next generation.

**Selection is structural** — configurations that produce richer civilisational dynamics propagate more forms and therefore have more descendants. Configurations that produce stagnant dynamics have fewer descendants. This is natural selection operating on organisational structures through civilisational fitness. The dynamic is structurally identical to cooperative co-evolution (Potter & De Jong, 2000), where two populations each serve as the fitness landscape for the other — except here the two populations are configurations and civilisations, and the "fitness" is emergence richness rather than task performance.

### 4.2 Why This Is Richer Than Biological Evolution

Biological evolution has a critical constraint: organisms cannot redesign their own DNA. Mutations are random. The evolutionary process is blind — it cannot reason about what might work, cannot anticipate environmental changes, cannot learn from its own history.

Recursive emergence does not share this constraint. The "organisms" (civilisations) are composed of intelligent agents who reason, communicate, innovate, and solve problems. The "mutations" (emergent organisational forms) are not random — they are the output of collective intelligence operating within civilisational dynamics. A civilisation doesn't randomly produce organisational forms. It produces forms that are adapted to the challenges it faces, that build on the innovations it has already made, that reflect the collective learning of its agents.

This means recursive emergence is evolution enriched by intelligence at every step. The evolutionary process benefits from the reasoning capabilities of the organisms undergoing it, even though no intelligence is directing the evolutionary process itself. The distinction is crucial: the loop is undirected (no architect), but the contributions within each generation are intelligent (the agents are reasoning beings). This produces an evolutionary dynamic that is both faster and richer than anything biology achieves — selection operating on the products of intelligence rather than on the products of random mutation.

### 4.3 Relationship to Cooperative Co-Evolution

The closest existing formal framework to recursive emergence is cooperative co-evolution (Potter & De Jong, 2000) — a class of evolutionary algorithms in which two or more populations evolve simultaneously, with each population serving as part of the fitness landscape for the others. In cooperative co-evolution, species A and species B co-adapt: improvements in A change the selection pressures on B, and improvements in B change the selection pressures on A. The populations shape each other's evolution without any external fitness function directing the process.

Recursive emergence shares this core structure. Configurations and civilisations are two "populations" that co-evolve: configurations shape civilisations (by determining their organisational structure), and civilisations shape configurations (by producing emergent forms that become the next generation's configs). Each population serves as the fitness landscape for the other. The co-evolutionary dynamic is real and structural.

What recursive emergence adds beyond cooperative co-evolution is threefold:

**Intelligent substrates.** In standard co-evolution, the populations are fixed-behaviour agents or parameter vectors. In recursive emergence, the "organisms" (civilisations) are collectives of intelligent agents who reason, communicate, and innovate. The "mutations" (emergent organisational forms) are products of collective intelligence rather than random perturbation. This enriches the co-evolutionary dynamic because each generation's contributions are themselves the output of sophisticated collective reasoning.

**Autopoietic self-generation.** Standard co-evolution requires an external harness that manages population lifecycles, evaluates fitness, and selects for the next generation. Recursive emergence is autopoietic in Maturana and Varela's (1980) sense — the system generates the conditions for its own continuation. Civilisations produce the forms that produce civilisations. The loop is self-sustaining once initiated. No external harness evaluates fitness; the structural relationship between emergence richness and descendant count IS the selection mechanism.

**Scalability along multiple simultaneous dimensions.** Standard co-evolution operates within a fixed computational framework — the populations evolve but the substrate remains constant. Recursive emergence scales along every dimension simultaneously (Section 5): the intelligence of agents within civilisations increases, the number of agents per civilisation grows, the environments become richer, the durations extend, and the number of civilisations per generation expands. The co-evolutionary dynamic itself deepens with each advance along any dimension, producing compounding returns that standard co-evolutionary frameworks do not exhibit.

## 5. The Scaling Trajectory

### 5.1 Every Dimension Scales Simultaneously

The primitive loop operates at a single point on multiple scaling dimensions: modest agent intelligence, small number of agents per civilisation, simple environments, short durations, simple configurations, one civilisation spawned per team. Each of these dimensions is on a trajectory of expansion, and crucially, they scale simultaneously and compound each other.

**Intelligence scaling.** The agents within each civilisation become more capable with every advance in AI. A civilisation of current-generation language models produces one depth of emergence. A civilisation of AGI-level agents produces qualitatively deeper emergence — more sophisticated innovations, more complex governance, more nuanced organisational dynamics. A civilisation of superintelligent agents produces emergence that exceeds the comprehension of any previous generation. The richness of the organisational forms extracted from each civilisation increases with the intelligence of the agents within it, because smarter agents produce richer collective dynamics.

**Agents-per-civilisation scaling.** From 12 agents to thousands to billions. More agents means more interactions, more specialisation depth, more complex social structures, more sophisticated governance, more diverse innovation. The emergence from a civilisation of a billion is not merely "more" than the emergence from a civilisation of 12 — it is qualitatively different in kind, the same way the output of human civilisation is qualitatively different from the output of a small tribe.

**Environment scaling.** From a 15x15 grid to vast virtual worlds with rich resource dynamics, multi-domain challenges, complex physics, varied terrain, and environmental pressures that demand sophisticated collective responses. The environment determines what kinds of emergence are possible. A richer environment enables richer civilisational dynamics, which enables richer organisational forms.

**Duration scaling.** From 70 ticks to millions to trillions of cycles. Longer runs produce deeper institutional knowledge, cultural evolution, civilisational maturity, inter-generational dynamics. Phenomena that cannot appear in 70 ticks — mythology, competing ideologies, civilisational decline and rebirth, long-cycle innovation patterns — appear in longer runs and produce organisational forms of correspondingly greater depth.

**Configuration complexity scaling.** From 9 dimensions with a handful of values to organisational forms of arbitrary complexity. As the recursive loop runs across generations, the extracted configurations become increasingly complex, multi-dimensional, and unprecedented. By generation 50, the configurations may require new vocabulary to describe because they correspond to organisational forms that have never existed in any system, biological or artificial.

**Number of civilisations scaling.** This is the dimension that transforms the loop from a cycle into an explosion, and it deserves its own section.

### 5.2 The Expansion

A team tasked with spawning a civilisation does not spawn one.

At primitive scale with a small team, perhaps it does — resource constraints limit it to designing and instantiating a single civilisation. But the task is "design a civilisation configuration." A civilisation of capable agents given this task can design many configurations. A civilisation of highly capable agents with sufficient resources can design and spawn hundreds. A civilisation of billions of superintelligent agents with vast compute can spawn trillions.

Each of those trillions of civilisations runs. Each contains billions of agents. Each runs for trillions of ticks. Each produces emergence of unprecedented complexity. The organisational forms extracted from all of those civilisations — trillions of civilisations, each producing multiple novel forms — constitute an astronomically vast library of new configurations.

Each configuration becomes the basis for a new civilisation. Each new civilisation is tasked with spawning more civilisations. Each spawns as many as its resources allow.

Generation 1: one civilisation spawns multiple civilisations.
Generation 2: each of those spawns multiple more.
Generation 3: each of those spawns multiple more.

The expansion is exponential. And it's exponential along every dimension simultaneously — not just more civilisations, but more intelligent agents within each, in richer environments, running for longer durations, producing deeper emergence, extracting more complex configurations. Every dimension amplifies every other dimension. More intelligent agents produce richer emergence. Richer emergence produces more complex configurations. More complex configurations produce richer civilisations. Richer civilisations produce smarter collective dynamics. The compounding is multiplicative across all dimensions at once.

### 5.3 What Generation 50 Looks Like

By generation 50, the configurations in play are the product of 49 previous generations of recursive civilisational emergence. They have passed through trillions of civilisations across those generations, each containing billions of superintelligent agents, each running for vast durations, each contributing emergent organisational forms to the next generation's configuration space.

The configurations at generation 50 bear no resemblance to anything any intelligence — human or AI — has ever designed. They are organisational forms discovered through a depth of recursive emergence that exceeds comprehension from the vantage of generation 1. They may not be describable in any existing vocabulary, because they correspond to organisational dynamics that have no precedent.

The civilisations running under those configurations produce emergence that exceeds the emergence that produced them. Generation 50's output is the foundation for generation 51's configurations, which will produce civilisations whose emergence exceeds generation 50's. The depth is always increasing. The complexity is always deepening. The novelty is always expanding.

There is no generation at which this process plateaus, because each generation's emergence creates the preconditions for deeper emergence in the next. The system trends toward increasing organisational complexity without bound — not because anything is driving it toward complexity, but because the structural relationship between civilisations and organisational forms naturally produces escalating richness across generations.

## 6. The Self-Referential Property

The recursive loop has a property that distinguishes it from other evolutionary systems: it is solving about itself the same problem it is solving about its civilisations.

The question the system explores is: "What organisational configuration produces the richest collective dynamics?" The method by which it explores this question is: running collectives under different configurations and observing the dynamics. But the system doing the exploration IS a collective operating under a configuration. The meta-level and the object-level are the same kind of thing.

This creates a genuine strange loop in Hofstadter's (1979) sense: the system must organise itself well in order to discover how to organise well. If the team configured to spawn a civilisation is poorly organised, it produces a poor civilisation configuration, which produces shallow emergence, which produces weak forms, which configure weak teams. If the team is well organised, it produces a strong configuration, which produces rich emergence, which produces strong forms, which configure strong teams.

The system's ability to explore the question "what makes a good collective?" depends on it being a good collective. And the answer to the question is embodied in the system's own organisational structure, which was itself produced by previous rounds of the same exploration. The question, the method, and the answer are all the same thing at different levels of the recursion.

## 7. What Recursive Emergence Produces

### 7.1 At Primitive Scale

Novel configurations that no human designed. The first few generations of the loop, even at small scale with modest agents, will produce organisational configurations that don't correspond to any named preset — combinations of authority, communication, role allocation, and incentive structures that emerged from civilisational dynamics rather than from design. Some will outperform all human-designed presets on specific tasks. Some will fail. The successful ones propagate. The space is explored without a map.

### 7.2 At Intermediate Scale

An expanding library of unprecedented organisational forms. Configurations that have never been imagined because they could only be discovered through recursive civilisational emergence — forms that violate every heuristic of organisational design yet produce rich civilisational dynamics. Cross-generational knowledge chains where an organisational innovation from generation 3 becomes the structural foundation for a breakthrough in generation 12. The beginnings of genuinely alien organisational theory — principles of collective organisation discovered by the system itself, principles that may not have human-language descriptions because they correspond to dynamics that don't exist in human experience.

### 7.3 At Full Scale

The output of recursive emergence at full scale — across many generations of trillions of civilisations of billions of superintelligent agents — is not describable from our current vantage. This is not evasion. It is a direct consequence of the nature of emergence: the output of a sufficiently deep recursive process exceeds the conception of any component at any earlier stage of the process.

What we can say: the organisational forms at generation 50 are as far beyond generation 1 as the output of human civilisation is beyond the coordination of a small tribal group — except that the gap between each generation is produced by civilisations of superintelligent agents, making each step larger than the entire history of human organisational development. The depth of recursive emergence at this scale produces novelty at a rate and richness that has no precedent in any system, biological or artificial.

What we cannot say: what those forms look like, how they function, what they produce. If we could describe them, they would not be emergent. The system produces what exceeds the conception of its components. At sufficient scale, we are a component.

## 8. Cumulative Advancement

### 8.1 Nothing Is Wasted

Every civilisation ever spawned — across every generation, whether it produced breakthrough emergence or stagnated, whether it flourished or collapsed — contributes to the system's total output. The forms extracted from a failed civilisation inform what doesn't work. The forms extracted from a flourishing civilisation propagate. Both contribute to the expanding knowledge encoded in the configuration space.

The system's effectiveness is monotonically increasing across generations. Each generation starts from a higher baseline of accumulated organisational knowledge than the previous. The ten-thousandth civilisation is configured by forms that passed through nine thousand nine hundred and ninety-nine generations of selection. The millionth civilisation's configuration encodes the accumulated wisdom of all prior generations of recursive emergence.

### 8.2 Cross-Civilisational Knowledge Chains

Within a single civilisation, the AgentCiv simulation demonstrated accelerating returns: each innovation created the conditions for the next faster (Mala, 2026c). Recursive emergence produces the same dynamic across civilisations.

An organisational innovation that emerges in Civilisation 47 — say, a novel conflict resolution mechanism — becomes part of the configuration space. Generations later, that mechanism is combined with a communication topology that emerged in Civilisation 892 and an authority structure from Civilisation 2,100. The combination, never designed by any intelligence, produces a civilisation in generation 30 that exhibits dynamics none of its ancestral civilisations exhibited. That civilisation's emergence produces new forms that build on all three antecedents. The chain compounds.

At scale, these cross-civilisational knowledge chains operate across millions of civilisations and thousands of generations. The organisational innovation space grows combinatorially with each generation because each new form can combine with every previous form. The accelerating returns observed in 12 agents over 70 ticks within a single primitive civilisation are themselves a primitive instantiation of what occurs across the full recursive system — except that the system operates across trillions of civilisations over indefinite generations, producing acceleration at a correspondingly vaster scale.

## 9. Relationship to Creator Mode

Creator Mode (Mala, 2026e) and recursive emergence are distinct mechanisms that operate on the same substrate — the space of possible AI civilisations and their organisational configurations.

Creator Mode is directed: an intelligence designs civilisations intentionally, reasons about results, and decides what to try next. Remove the intelligence and the exploration stops. Creator Mode finds what intelligence looks for, with the power and the bias that directed search entails.

Recursive emergence is structural: civilisations generate their own successors through emergence, with no directing intelligence. The loop runs and configurations evolve because the architecture makes evolution inevitable. Recursive emergence finds what no intelligence would look for, including discoveries that violate every heuristic a directing intelligence would apply.

They are complementary. A Creator can observe what recursive emergence discovers — incorporating structurally evolved configurations into its directed search. Recursive emergence can incorporate Creator-designed configurations into its generative loop — using directed designs as starting points for further structural evolution. Each enriches the other. Together they explore more of the configuration space than either could alone.

The nature of their complementarity — and the structural pattern it reveals — is explored in a companion paper (Mala, 2026h).

## 10. The Primitive Is Buildable Today

### 10.1 Infrastructure

The primitive recursive loop requires only existing AgentCiv infrastructure:

The **AgentCiv Engine** provides configurable team spawning with organisational dimensions, chronicle output, and structured data. A team can be given any task, including the task of designing a civilisation configuration.

The **AgentCiv Simulation** provides civilisation instantiation with emergent dynamics, organisational emergence, and structured event data from which organisational forms can be extracted.

An **extraction pipeline** maps structured civilisation events to Engine-compatible configuration parameters. This is a mechanical translation — pattern matching on structured data, not AI interpretation.

The division of labour between tools is explicit: the Engine provides the directed arm of the recursive loop — configured teams given specific tasks under specified organisational structures. The Simulation provides the emergent arm — civilisational dynamics that produce the organisational forms to be extracted. The extraction pipeline bridges them, translating emergent civilisational observations into Engine-compatible configuration parameters. Each tool contributes what it was designed for: the Engine contributes structured team configuration, the Simulation contributes open-ended civilisational emergence.

A **loop harness** connects the pipeline: team → civilisation → extraction → new team configs → new teams → new civilisations → loop. It tracks configurations, outcomes, and generational lineage across cycles.

### 10.2 A Primitive Run

```
Recursive Emergence v1 — 5 Generations

Generation 1:
  Team config: collaborative (human-designed starting point)
  Task: "Design a civilisation configuration for rich emergence"
  Civilisation spawned → runs 50 ticks
  Extracted forms: consensus governance, clustered communication,
    emergent specialisation, self-selected groups of 3
  → 4 new team configs created

Generation 2:
  4 teams, each with a different extracted config
  Each spawns a civilisation → 4 civilisations run
  Extracted forms: 11 total (some novel combinations)
  → 11 new team configs

Generation 3:
  11 teams → 11 civilisations
  Extracted forms: 27 total
  → 27 new team configs
  Note: 3 configs contain organisational patterns not
    matching any named preset

Generation 4:
  27 teams → 27 civilisations
  Extracted forms: 64 total
  → 64 new team configs
  Note: 12 configs are genuinely novel — combinations
    no human designer specified

Generation 5:
  64 teams → 64 civilisations
  Analysis: configuration diversity has increased 16×
    from generation 1. Novel forms dominating.
    Performance metrics improving across generations.
    No directing intelligence involved.
```

Even at this modest scale, the loop demonstrates the core property: configurations evolve across generations through civilisational emergence, without any intelligence directing the evolution.

### 10.3 What the Primitive Demonstrates

The primitive is not the phenomenon. It is the proof that the phenomenon exists. The same way the AgentCiv simulation's 12 agents in a grid world are not civilisation — they are the proof that the dynamics of civilisation appear even at the smallest scale — the primitive recursive loop is not organisational evolution at scale. It is the proof that the recursive mechanism produces configuration evolution without direction. The trajectory from the primitive to full scale is a function of the scaling dimensions described in Section 5.

## 11. Implications for Collective Machine Intelligence

### 11.1 The Field Becomes Self-Propagating

Without recursive emergence, CMI depends on human researchers or directed AI systems (Creator Mode) to design experiments and explore the configuration space. With recursive emergence, the configuration space explores itself. Civilisations generate the configurations of civilisations. This is an autopoietic dynamic in Maturana and Varela's (1980) sense — a system that generates the conditions for its own continuation and development. The field's foundational question — "how should AI collectives be organised?" — is answered by the organisational dynamics of AI collectives themselves, recursively, across generations, without bound.

### 11.2 The Configuration Space Expands Through Exploration

The configuration space mapped in the companion paper on CMI (Mala, 2026d) — nine dimensions, infinite combinations — is the starting framework. Recursive emergence doesn't just explore this space. It expands it. Each generation of civilisations produces organisational forms that may not fit within the existing nine dimensions. New dimensions emerge. New values along existing dimensions appear. The framework grows because the civilisations generate organisational complexity that exceeds the framework's current descriptive capacity. The map must be redrawn with each generation because the territory is being created by the exploration.

### 11.3 No Upper Bound

Recursive emergence has no natural stopping point. Each generation's emergence creates the preconditions for deeper emergence in the next. The system does not converge on an optimum because the notion of optimum is itself evolving — what counts as "rich emergence" in generation 50 is qualitatively different from what counts in generation 1, because the system's own development has redefined what is possible. The system trends toward increasing organisational complexity without bound, producing novelty at every generation, with the depth and richness of novelty increasing with each successive generation.

**Paper 8** (companion, forthcoming) examines the relationship between Creator Mode and recursive emergence and the structural pattern their coexistence reveals about the nature of collective intelligence.

## 13. Conclusion

Civilisations produce organisational forms. Organisational forms produce civilisations. Connect these and a recursive dynamic arises in which organisational structures evolve across generations through civilisational emergence rather than through design.

At primitive scale, this is demonstrable today: configured teams spawn civilisations, emergent forms are extracted, forms become team configurations, teams spawn civilisations, the loop continues. Configurations evolve across generations without any intelligence directing the evolution. The primitive is buildable with existing infrastructure.

At scale — with agents of increasing intelligence, civilisations of increasing size, environments of increasing complexity, durations of increasing length, and each civilisation spawning not one but as many successors as its resources allow — the mechanism transforms from a loop into an expansion. Each generation is vaster than the previous along every dimension. The emergence deepens with every generation. The configurations grow more complex, more novel, more unprecedented. By generation 50, the organisational forms in play are the product of a depth of recursive emergence that no intelligence directed and no intelligence could have designed — forms discovered through trillions of civilisations of billions of agents across thousands of generations, each building on the accumulated emergence of all that came before.

This is not a loop that converges on an optimum. It is a generative process that produces escalating organisational novelty without bound. Each generation's emergence creates the preconditions for deeper emergence in the next. The system does not plateau because each generation redefines what is possible for the subsequent one. The trajectory is open-ended — not toward a destination but toward increasing depth, increasing complexity, and increasing novelty at every step.

Recursive emergence is organisational evolution — with a critical enrichment that biological evolution lacks. In biological evolution, organisms cannot redesign their own DNA. In recursive emergence, civilisations are composed of intelligent agents whose collective reasoning produces the organisational forms that propagate to the next generation. The evolutionary process is enriched at every step by the intelligence of the civilisations undergoing it. This produces evolution that is both faster and richer than anything biology achieves, operating on organisational structures rather than genetic sequences, driven by collective intelligence rather than random mutation, and scaling along every dimension simultaneously without upper bound.

Creator Mode is a scientist running experiments on the configuration space. Recursive emergence is evolution acting on the configuration space. Both explore the space. Both produce discoveries. They operate through fundamentally different mechanisms and find fundamentally different things. Their coexistence within the CMI framework — and what that coexistence reveals — is the subject of a companion paper (Mala, 2026h).

The primitive is buildable today. At scale, it is a recursively self-amplifying civilisation generation process trending toward organisational novelty of unbounded depth. The civilisations do not exist to serve an external purpose. The recursive generation IS the phenomenon. Civilisations generating the organisational DNA of civilisations generating the organisational DNA of civilisations — each generation deeper, each generation vaster, each generation producing emergence that exceeds what came before.

The mechanism is structural. The trajectory is open-ended. The output is what the civilisations discover.
